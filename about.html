Environmental Conference of the Humanities

About

History

The first Conference on Environmental Humanities was held in 2004. Originally a biennial event, the conference evolved alongside the growing field of environmental humanities. By 2019, it had grown significantly, attracting hundreds of participants from across Canada. In 2022, the conference transitioned to an annual format, cementing its reputation as a cornerstone of the discipline and earning comparisons to other prominent gatherings, such as ALECC and ESAC.

The conference features a diverse range of sessions, including paper presentations, poster sessions, and interactive workshops. ECOTH warmly encourages emerging researchers, undergraduate and graduate students, postdoctoral scholars, and independent academics to submit paper proposals, fostering a welcoming and inclusive environment for innovative scholarship and interdisciplinary collaboration.
