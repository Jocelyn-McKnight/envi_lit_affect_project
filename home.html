Hosted by the University of Lethbridge, the Environmental Conference of the Humanities (ECOTH) is an annual gathering that brings together scholars, researchers, and students with a shared interest in exploring the intersection of environment studies and the humanities. This conference provides a platform for interdisciplinary dialogue, fostering innovative approaches to understanding the relationship between human culture and the natural world.

ECOTH embraces the study of all aspects of the environment through a humanist lens, addressing topics such as ecocriticism, environmental history, environmental philosophy and ethics, environmental anthropology, political ecology, posthumanism, animal studies, and much more. Additionally, the conference highlights emerging areas of inquiry, including ecocultural studies and the cultural implications of climate change.

By encouraging diverse perspectives and approaches, ECOTH aims to deepen our understanding of environmental issues and inspire creative, ethical, and inclusive responses to the challenges facing our planet.
